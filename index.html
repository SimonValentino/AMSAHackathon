GENERAL INFO

What is AMSA Code Bash?

AMSA Code Bash is a hackathon for all high school students in Massachusetts, where they come together to work in teams on a computer science project and compete for prizes. Hackathons are great ways for aspiring engineers to learn and grow.

How do I participate?

AMSA Code Bash is going to be an in-person event at the Advanced Math and Science Academy Charter School (201 Forest St, Marlborough, MA 01752). Admission to the event will cost $10 dollars and will require that you sign up using the "Sign Up" button at the top of this page. See the agenda for specific event times.

What do I need to bring?

It is highly recommended that you bring your own laptop and charger, as the laptops at AMSA have restrictions on them that prevent students from accessing every recourse on the machine. Other than that, everything from food to materials will be provided.

Teams?

Yes, you will be working in teams. Teams will be groups of 2-4 students, and cannot be changed after the project ideas and team names have been submitted. If you do not have any teammates going into the hackathon, that's ok! There will be time to find and form teams at the event.

CODE OF CONDUCT

AMSA Code Bash is an event about empowering high school students to collaborate on computing projects that are personally meaningful and self directed. We are committed to providing a safe and welcoming space, without regard to gender, race, ethnicity, nationality, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, mental or physical disability, physical appearance, neuro(a)typicality, religion, level of knowledge, level of experience, socioeconomic status or background, political affiliation, or any other attribute.

As organizers of this event, we believe that the below principles are essential to maintaining this environment. In addition, we believe that they are good principles for life in general. This Code of Conduct serves to ensure that everybody and anybody who wishes to participate is able to do so.

Do Not Harass
Harassment is any unwelcome or hostile behavior towards another person for any reason. This includes, but is not limited to, offensive verbal comments related to personal characteristics or choices, deliberate intimidation, bullying, stalking, following, harassing photography or recording, sustained disruption of discussion or events, nonconsensual publication of private comments, inappropriate physical contact, or unwelcome sexual attention. Conduct need not be intentional to be harassment.

Respect the Opinions and Abilities of Others
AMSA Code Bash is designed to be a place where people of different skill levels and approaches work together toward common goals. As a result, we do not expect that everybody will share the same opinion. However, we do expect that disagreement is done respectfully.

Additionally, we expect that members will educate others respectfully. To this end, do not assume anybody else’s level of expertise or knowledge. Do not belittle a lack of information, or insist on unnecessary precision. We are all learning, so afford others—as well as yourself—room to grow.

If you are unable to resolve an issue, or are uncomfortable doing so, you should contact one of the organizers, either in person or by email (gtheos@amsacs.org, cpanara@amsacs.org).

This code of conduct is adapted from the Civic Tech DC Code of Conduct.
